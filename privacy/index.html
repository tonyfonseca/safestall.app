Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 29, 2026

The short version

Safestall has no accounts and no tracking, and your location is never stored or shared. We use one privacy-first analytics tool (TelemetryDeck) to see which features get used — it records anonymous events that can't be traced back to you, and you can turn it off in Settings. The other exception is feedback you choose to send on a listing (thumbs up/down) — that sends a permanent random identifier so duplicate reports can be recognized, and nothing else about you. Details below.

About Safestall

Safestall is a free, non-commercial iOS app that helps you find safe, gender-neutral restrooms nearby. It's a passion project — there is no business model, no ads, and no monetization.

Location data

Safestall uses your device's location solely to find restrooms near you. Here is exactly what happens with your coordinates:

  • Refuge Restrooms — your approximate latitude and longitude (rounded to ~100 m) are sent to refugerestrooms.org to query their restroom database.
  • OpenStreetMap (Overpass API) — your approximate latitude and longitude are sent to overpass-api.de to query for nearby gender-neutral restroom nodes.
  • Apple MapKit — the app uses Apple's on-device MapKit framework to display the map, to enrich listings with business names and hours via Apple Places, and to load street-level imagery (Look Around) for a listing you select. MapKit requests are handled by iOS itself and are subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • Restroom summaries — to show a short summary of a listing's community notes, the app fetches a pre-computed summary from a read-only Safestall server (hosted on Supabase), sending only the public IDs of the restrooms in your current map area — never your location, identity, or device. If that server is unavailable, supported devices summarize the (already-public) notes on-device instead, and otherwise the original notes show. Nothing about you is collected or stored.

Your location is used in real time and is never stored, logged, or sent to a server we operate — your approximate coordinates go only to the third-party services listed above. The one Safestall-operated server the app talks to (for restroom summaries, above) receives restroom IDs, not your location. No personal information beyond approximate coordinates is included in any location request.

When you ask Siri or run a Shortcut, Apple handles your request. Safestall uses your location once, in memory, to answer — nothing is stored or shared, and the app never hands your restroom searches to Spotlight or Siri Suggestions.

No accounts

Safestall does not require or offer user accounts. There is no sign-up, no login, and no profile.

Analytics

Safestall uses TelemetryDeck, a privacy-first analytics service, to see which features people use so we can make the app better. It records simple, anonymous events — things like "opened a listing's detail" or "tapped the map-style toggle." It never records your location, the specific restrooms you look at, an account (there isn't one), or anything that identifies you.

TelemetryDeck is built to stay anonymous: the identifier it uses is scrambled (hashed) twice — once on your device and again on its server — so neither TelemetryDeck nor we can ever tie events back to you. Its servers are in the EU. You can turn this off any time in Settings under "Share anonymous usage."

That is the only analytics SDK we use — no Firebase, no Mixpanel, no Amplitude, no Segment. We do not use ad networks or attribution SDKs, and we do not use crash reporting SDKs that collect device identifiers. Apple's built-in App Analytics (anonymous, off by default per your device settings) may also be available; we have no access to individual-level data through it.

Third-party services

Safestall communicates with the following services. When the app looks up restrooms, each receives only the approximate location data described above — no user profiles and no personal information. The one exception is community feedback you choose to send, described under the enrichment server below.

  • Refuge Restrooms (refugerestrooms.org) — community-maintained open dataset. Privacy info.
  • OpenStreetMap / Overpass API (overpass-api.de) — open geographic data. Privacy policy.
  • Apple MapKit / Apple Places — on-device framework provided by iOS. Privacy policy.
  • Safestall enrichment server (Supabase) — a server we operate that returns pre-computed restroom metadata (currently short summaries of public community notes, whether a listing is still listed, and a corrected display name), looked up by the public IDs of the listings in your map area. Those lookups send only those IDs and your IP address — no account, precise location, or anything else about you. If you choose to give feedback on a listing (the thumbs up/down on its detail), the report sends that listing's public ID, your feedback, and a permanent random identifier this app generates — so duplicate reports can be recognized. That identifier is not your name, account, device ID, or location (there are none of those); it is the only thing that persists between your reports.
  • TelemetryDeck (analytics) — a privacy-first analytics service that records anonymous feature-usage events (described under Analytics above). It receives an event name and a double-hashed identifier that can't be traced back to you — never your location, account, or which restrooms you view. EU-hosted; you can turn it off in Settings.

Beyond the privacy-first services listed above, we do not share data with ad networks, data brokers, or anyone else. We don't sell data, and there are no ads. The only data the app sends anywhere is what is described above.

Accuracy of listings

The restroom listings come from Refuge Restrooms and OpenStreetMap — open databases that real people add to over time. We don't write, own, or check these listings. We just help you find them.

So some of it might be wrong, out of date, or missing. A listing showing up here doesn't guarantee the restroom exists, is open, is accessible, or is safe right now.

Use your own judgment — especially when it comes to your safety. If something feels off, trust yourself over the app.

On-device data

Safestall stores a small amount of data locally on your device using standard iOS storage. None of it ever leaves your device:

  • Preferences — settings like your chosen map style and app icon.
  • Cached restroom results — recently fetched listings, kept so the app loads faster and works offline.
  • Last map position — the area you were last viewing, so the map can open there instantly on your next visit instead of loading from scratch. It's an approximate location, kept only on your device.
  • Recent destinations — the last few places you tapped in search (their names and map coordinates), so you can get back to them quickly. You can remove these anytime with the Clear button next to Recent in search.

Deleting the app deletes all of this data.

On-device summaries

On supported devices, Apple's on-device language model may summarize the community notes from a listing's duplicate entries into a short blurb. This runs entirely on your device — the notes (already public Refuge data) are never sent anywhere, and no new data is collected.

Children's privacy

Safestall does not collect personal information from anyone, including children. The app requires only location permission to function and stores nothing about its users.

Consent

Safestall requests location access through the standard iOS permission prompt. You can revoke this permission at any time in Settings > Privacy & Security > Location Services > Safestall. Without location access, the app cannot show nearby restrooms.

Because Safestall does not store your location or any personal data, there is no data to delete. Revoking location permission immediately stops all location use.

Your rights

Because we don't collect or store personal data, there is nothing for us to provide, correct, or delete. If you have questions or concerns, you're welcome to reach out.
